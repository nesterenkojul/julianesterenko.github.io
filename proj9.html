Infographics redesign for Ekaterina Schulmann's book

In 2024 russian political scientist Ekaterina Schulmann released a book titled "The Return of the State. Russia in the 2000s" which offers a backed-up-by-data analysis of the transformation of russian state and society during the "fat" 2000s. It also allows one to trace back the roots of the current destructive authoritarian regime. As a huge fan of the author's work, I of course read this one too. Despite being very interested in the contents of the book, I was notably upset by the graphics there. The book is backed up by a lot of data from different (mainly governmental) resources and charts used to represent that data is also borrowed from those sources. As a result, the book's visuals are stylistically incoherent and on many occasions they simply break the basic dataviz rules, which leads to a complete miscommunication with the reader.

Consequently, I've embarked on a journey to redesign those infographics â€“ with a goal to optimize the book's message delivery and improve the overall reading experience. Inspiration also comes from the thought that the main audience of the book are people with the same moral values and political views as me (anti-war, anti-current-regime russians concerned with politics), so it's kind of a way for me to channel my appreciation and support for that community with the (highly specific) skills and knowledge I have.

Everything is compiled in this doc (in russian).
